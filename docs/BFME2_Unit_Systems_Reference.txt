1. BATTALION (HORDE) SYSTEM
================================================================================

ORGANIZATION
  The fundamental controllable unit is the battalion (called "horde" internally).
  A battalion is a group of individual soldiers that move and fight as one
  selectable entity. Only the battalion leader needs to be selected to control
  the entire group.

BATTALION SIZES BY UNIT TYPE
  Heavy Infantry (Swords/Pikes) .... 15-20
  Archers .......................... 10-15
  Cavalry .......................... 6-10
  Siege units, Trolls, Monsters .... 1 (single entity)

  Specific examples:
    Gondor Soldiers ........... 15
    Gondor Archers ............ 15
    Gondor Knights ............ 10
    Mordor Orc Fighters ....... 20
    Mordor Orc Archers ........ 15
    Goblin Fighters/Archers ... 15
    Rohirrim .................. 10

INI STRUCTURE (HordeContain)
  Each battalion is defined by a Behavior = HordeContain block. Key parameters:
    - Slots: Maximum number of soldiers
    - InitialPayload: Unit type and count spawned on training
    - RankInfo: Formation positions (X/Y coords per soldier per rank)
    - FrontAngle: Front facing arc (270° typical, 360° = immune to flanking)
    - FlankedDelay: Time in ms before flanking penalty applies (~2000)
    - MeleeBehavior = Amoeba: How soldiers behave in melee
    - RanksToReleaseWhenAttacking: How many ranks break formation to engage
    - BannerCarrierPosition: Where banner carrier stands
    - PassengerFilter: Which unit types can be in this horde

INDIVIDUAL SOLDIER DEATH & BATTALION DEGRADATION
  - Each soldier has individual HP. When a soldier dies, the battalion loses
    that soldier visually and functionally.
  - The battalion continues with reduced numbers — fewer soldiers means less
    total damage output and fewer bodies to absorb hits.
  - Arrows/projectiles are distributed across living soldiers, so larger
    battalions are more durable per-soldier against ranged fire.
  - The battalion is destroyed only when ALL soldiers die.
  - No morale-based rout mechanic in vanilla — battalions fight to the last
    man unless affected by Fear/Terror abilities.

BATTALION REGENERATION
  - Banner Carriers (upgrade): Battalions at Level 2+ gradually regenerate
    dead soldiers (~6.5s per soldier near fortress, ~10s near Wells).
  - Wells / Healing Structures: Standing near heals at 2-3% HP/sec and
    respawns dead members.
  - Heal Spellbook Power: Heals group, restores one dead member per battalion.
  - Healing rate is percentage-based (not flat HP).

COMBO HORDES (BATTALION MERGING)
  Two compatible battalions can merge by right-clicking one on the other.
  Post-patch 1.03 bonuses:
    Melee + Ranged:  +10% damage, +10 armor
    Melee + Cavalry: +20% armor
    Pike + Ranged:   +120% damage
    Pike + Melee:    +120% damage

  Merging shares veterancy level (higher applies) and upgrades apply at
  single-battalion price. However, formations are lost, combo cannot be
  un-merged, and the melee component in melee+ranged combos stays defensive.

2. FORMATION SYSTEM
================================================================================

HOW FORMATIONS WORK
  Formations are alternate horde definitions. When clicking the formation
  toggle (TOGGLE_FORMATION), the game swaps between entirely different
  HordeContain blocks with different soldier positions, stat modifiers, and
  properties. Cycle: Line -> Wedge -> Shield Wall/Porcupine -> back to Line.

AVAILABLE FORMATIONS

  LINE FORMATION (Default)
    - Standard spread-out arrangement
    - No stat modifiers for standard hordes
    - Combo hordes: +25% attack, -25% armor
    - Best general-purpose formation

  WEDGE FORMATION (Offensive)
    - V-shaped offensive formation
    - +25% attack bonus, -25% armor penalty
    - Designed for aggressive engagements and charges
    - Cavalry wedge: +25% damage, -25% armor

  BLOCK / SHIELD WALL / DEFENSIVE FORMATION
    - Tight defensive grouping
    - +25% armor bonus, -25% speed penalty (up to -40% for some factions)
    - Ideal for holding ground or absorbing ranged fire

  PORCUPINE FORMATION (Pikemen Only)
    - Two concentric circles with pikes extended outward
    - +65% armor bonus
    - Units CANNOT move and barely attack
    - Massive crush deceleration — stops cavalry trampling dead
      (except Ents, Demolishers, crushing monsters)
    - Patch 1.05: also grants +25% damage and +25% armor

  CAVALRY CHARGE FORMATION
    - Speed +25%, Damage +50%, Armor -50%
    - Designed for hit-and-run trampling attacks

FORMATION AVAILABILITY
  Infantry (Swords):  Line, Wedge, Shield Wall/Block
  Pikemen:            Line, Wedge, Porcupine
  Cavalry:            Line, Wedge, Charge
  Archers:            Line, Wedge (limited options)
  Siege/Monsters:     No formations

ARMY-LEVEL FORMATIONS (Planning Mode)
  Hold both mouse buttons and drag to create a formation shape for multiple
  selected battalions. The army arranges itself into the drawn shape.

3. MOVEMENT SYSTEM
================================================================================

BATTALION MOVEMENT
  Each unit type has a Locomotor (locomotor.ini) controlling:
    Speed, Acceleration, Braking, TurnTime, TurnThreshold,
    ChargeAvailable, ChargeSpeed, ZAxisBehavior, StickToGround

TERRAIN & PATHABILITY
  Locomotor Surfaces parameter defines traversable terrain:
    GROUND ........ Standard terrain
    RUBBLE ........ Destroyed building debris
    WATER ......... Shallow water (up to WadeWaterDepth, ~5 feet)
    DEEP_WATER .... Ship-navigable depth (6+ feet)
    CLIFF ......... Vertical surfaces (Shelob only)
    OBSTACLE ...... Ruins/obstacles (Mumakil, monsters, Army of Dead)
    AIR ........... Fly anywhere (Fellbeasts, Eagles)

  The engine does NOT have granular terrain-type speed modifiers (no forest
  or swamp penalties). Terrain restriction is binary: passable or not.

GROUP MOVEMENT
  - Formation Move (hold both mouse buttons + drag): All selected units move
    at the speed of the SLOWEST unit, maintaining relative positions.
  - Without formation move, each battalion moves at its own speed.
  - ~1 second input delay before movement (peer-to-peer lockstep networking).

PATHFINDING & BLOCKING
  Grid-based pathfinding (notoriously imperfect).
    - Infantry blocks infantry
    - Cavalry blocks cavalry
    - Cavalry can block infantry (and vice versa)
  This blocking is a core tactical mechanic — cheap units can physically
  block enemy movement.

WAYPOINTS & PATROL
  - Alt + Right-Click: Sets waypoints for complex paths
  - Right-clicking on a set waypoint creates a patrol loop
  - Planning Mode (Z key): Queue succession of orders, execute all at once

4. STANCE SYSTEM
================================================================================

Three stances available to all units and heroes (except siege/monsters).

AGGRESSIVE STANCE (Hotkey: D)
  Attack:  +50%
  Armor:   -25%
  Vision:  Larger field of vision in movement direction
  Auto-Acquire: Yes — chase any enemy in LOS until enemy leaves or dies
  Pursuit: Unlimited within LOS
  Best For: Attacking buildings, flanking, finishing retreating enemies,
            cavalry charges into archers

BATTLE STANCE (Hotkey: F) — Default
  Attack:  No modifier
  Armor:   No modifier
  Auto-Acquire: Yes — engage at medium range, chase briefly
  Pursuit: Short chase distance, stops when enemy leaves LOS
  Best For: General combat, balanced behavior

HOLD GROUND / DEFENSIVE STANCE (Hotkey: G)
  Attack:  Decreased
  Armor:   +25%
  Vision:  -90% (effectively blind to non-adjacent threats)
  Auto-Acquire: No — only fight back if directly attacked
  Pursuit: None — units stay put
  Best For: Holding under ranged fire, cavalry post-trample survival,
            screening while moving through enemy territory

STRATEGIC NOTES
  - Stances are NOT advantageous long-term. Aggressive vs. Battle stance
    head-on: Aggressive loses because armor debuff outweighs attack buff.
  - Stances are for SPECIFIC TACTICAL MOMENTS: switch to Aggressive for a
    flank, Defensive to absorb a charge, then return to Battle stance.
  - Cavalry micro: Aggressive before trample, immediately Defensive after
    charge to survive deceleration phase and crush revenge damage.

5. CONTROL SYSTEM
================================================================================

BASIC COMMANDS
  Right-Click (ground) ...... Move to location
  Right-Click (enemy) ....... Attack target
  Right-Click (building) .... Garrison / interact
  A + Left-Click ............ Attack-Move (move toward destination, engage
                              enemies along the path using target priorities)
  G ......................... Guard (follow and protect target unit/building)
  X ......................... Scatter (spread out, dodge AoE abilities)
  S ......................... Stop all current orders

CONTROL GROUPS
  Ctrl + Number (0-9) ....... Assign selected units to numbered group
  Number key ................ Select that control group
  Alt + Number .............. Jump camera to group location

SELECTION
  Left-Click ................ Select single unit
  Left-Click + Drag ......... Box select multiple units
  E (once) .................. Select all same type nearby
  E (twice) ................. Select all same type on map
  Q ......................... Select all fighting units
  O ......................... Select all heroes
  Ctrl + H .................. Cycle through heroes
  H ......................... Jump to citadel

SHIFT-QUEUING & WAYPOINTS
  Shift + Selection ......... Add units to current selection
  Alt + Right-Click ......... Add waypoint for multi-point pathing
  Right-click waypoint ...... Create patrol loop
  Shift + Unit production ... Queue 5 units from building

PLANNING MODE
  Z ......................... Queue multiple orders, execute simultaneously

RALLY POINTS
  Click production building, right-click map to set rally point.

6. SPECIAL BEHAVIORS
================================================================================

AUTO-ACQUIRE TARGETS
  - Controlled by HordeAIUpdate with AutoAcquireEnemiesWhenIdle
  - Range tied to unit's Vision range
  - Target priority defined by combat chains (ArcherCombatChain, etc.)
    assigning priority values per target type
  - Archers do NOT auto-attack buildings by default
  - Aggressive expands auto-acquire; Defensive disables it

CAVALRY TRAMPLE / CHARGE MECHANICS
  - Cavalry have a deceleration stat controlling trample count per charge
  - Each trample deals CRUSH damage + knockdown
  - Below deceleration threshold: cavalry stop trampling, move slower than
    basic infantry — vulnerable to being chased/flanked
  - Crush Revenge Damage: Cavalry take damage when trampling.
    Pikemen = near-instant death. Archers/swords = minimal revenge damage.
  - Escape + re-enter at speed = trample again
  - Trampling from behind avoids most pike revenge damage
  - Crush FlankingBonus = 200% (3x from flanks/rear)

PIKE/SPEAR BRACING (PORCUPINE)
  - Primary anti-cavalry tool
  - +65% armor, massive crush deceleration
  - Countered by: ranged fire, Ents, Demolishers, crushing monsters
  - Tactical combo: Activate Porcupine just before cavalry impact,
    switch to Aggressive after to damage stopped cavalry

RANGED UNIT BEHAVIOR
  - Archers must STOP moving to fire (no fire-while-moving)
  - Defined firing arcs (NaturalTurretAngle, TurretMaxDeflection)
  - AcceptableAimDelta: precision required before firing
  - Edge-of-range pathfinding issues (back-and-forth shuffling)
  - Longshot (Rangers ability): Targeted volley on location
  - Mirkwood Archers: Longest range + auto-stealth when stationary

FEAR / TERROR SYSTEM
  Four crowd-control categories:
    Fear:     Enemies cower on floor (e.g., Boromir's Horn)
    Terror:   Enemies flee in panic (e.g., Nazgul Screech)
    Paralyze: Complete stun, uncounterable
    Slow:     Speed = 0, only countered by specific anti-category

  Countered by Fear Resistance (Leadership abilities, Spellbook Horns,
  high veterancy). Level 5 units = full immunity.

FLANKING
  Standard weapons:  +50% damage (1.5x multiplier)
  Crush weapons:     +200% damage (3x multiplier)
  FlankedDelay:      ~2000ms before flanking penalty kicks in
  FrontAngle:        Arc counting as "front" (270° typical, 360° = immune)

DAMAGE TYPES
  SLASH ......... Sword infantry
  PIERCE ........ Bow infantry
  SPECIALIST .... Pikemen and spear-throwers
  CRUSH ......... Cavalry trampling, large monsters
  HERO .......... Melee hero attacks
  HERO_RANGED ... Ranged hero attacks
  FLAME ......... Fire upgrades, siege weapons
  LOGICAL_FIRE .. Burning ground damage-over-time
  SIEGE ......... Siege weapons vs structures
  MAGIC ......... Magical abilities
  CAVALRY ....... Mounted unit melee attacks
  REFLECTED ..... ReflectDamage module damage
  STRUCTURAL .... Building interaction damage
  META .......... Physics-based damage (catapults vs Mumakil, wing flaps)

  Each unit has an armor set with percentage resistance per damage type
  (higher % = MORE damage taken).

7. WALL / SIEGE INTEGRATION
================================================================================

WALL TYPES
  Standard Walls: Built by Dwarves and Men. Can have Gates, Towers,
    and Catapult emplacements on wall segments.
  Rampart Walls: Broad walkways on fortress maps. Archers and siege
    can stand on top with height/range advantage.

WALL GARRISON
  - Archers garrison in wall towers and dormitory expansions
  - Dormitory expansions hold up to 3 units
  - Garrisoned units gain height advantage (range boost)

WALL SCALING
  - Goblins can scale standard walls
  - Siege Ladders (Isengard) and Siege Towers (Mordor) bypass ramparts
  - Siege Towers cannot pass through rampart gates (height restriction)

SIEGE UNIT TARGETING
  Battering Rams:   Only attack gates and buildings (not wall segments)
  Catapults/Trebs:  AoE damage, effective vs units and structures
  Demolishers:      Super-effective vs buildings, can deploy for +armor
  Meta Damage:      Siege units extremely vulnerable — flying units with
                    wing flap can destroy in one hit

8. HERO INTERACTION
================================================================================

HEROES AS INDEPENDENT UNITS
  Heroes are single-entity units (horde size 1) operating independently.
  They do NOT embed inside battalions in vanilla BFME2.

LEADERSHIP AURAS
  Heroes with Leadership provide area-of-effect buffs:
    Fear Resistance (RESIST_FEAR)
    Experience gain: +100% (2x multiplier)
    Armor: +50%
    Damage: x1.5 (150%)

  Implemented via AttributeModifierAuraUpdate targeting +CAVALRY +INFANTRY
  within set range, ~2.5s refresh.

  Leadership does NOT stack: Multiple leadership buffs replace each other
  (longest-duration wins). Spellbook powers stack independently.

HERO-HORDE UNITS (Rise of the Witch King expansion)
  Combined hero/battalion entities — hero leads an attached battalion,
  trained as a single unit.

BANNER CARRIERS
  - Purchased upgrade for a battalion
  - Immediately promotes to Level 2
  - Displays veterancy level visually
  - Enables soldier regeneration near healing structures
  - Provides experience bonus to nearby troops

EXPERIENCE / VETERANCY
  Regular units: Level 1-5. Heroes: Level 1-10.
  Each level grants ~+15% damage and +15% health (regular units).
  Banner carriers: +20 HP/level (max +80), +5 base damage/level (max +20).
  Level 5 = full fear immunity.
  Experience boosted by Leadership auras (+100% gain rate).
